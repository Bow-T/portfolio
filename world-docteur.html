Docteur Sirop

A doctor who speaks softly — so a hospital feels a little less frightening to a child.

scroll to begin
The problem
For a hospitalized child, the scariest part is rarely the illness.

It's the waiting. The strange machines. The grown-ups who talk over them instead of to them. A fear that no one quite takes the time to explain away — until a small patient stops asking, and simply goes quiet.

The journey
So we built a companion — not another form to fill in.

Sirop is a deer in a white coat the child can actually talk to, out loud, in real time. Every day starts with a gentle mood check-in. When things get heavy there's an espace zen to breathe. And the lessons are adventures, not pamphlets — the child's own body becomes the world they explore.
